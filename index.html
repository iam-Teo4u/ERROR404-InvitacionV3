Shadows for the win.

Poor text here is in a dark place, so why don't we just light it up a bit? When I mean "light it up", I mean putting literal lights along with it, so not only it won't feel alone, but it will give it this little neat effect!
You know, it looks like a cyberpunk movie - when the screens all around the city are so bright they blur out the picture. Well, it's practically the same effect here: instead of leaving your pixels sharp and ugly over a simple black background, add a bit of shadows on the most most important elements so it won't be that dark after all!

Night Mode already enabled.

Who else is always using Twitter - or any website, really - in Night Mode? You too?! Thank goodness I'm not the only one!
Bright tones are always more eye-scratching than dark ones, so use brightness as a way to make your text feel important! But what if you needed to grab your visitor's attention even more? That's when colors come in. Don't worry about blasting hyper-saturated colors: the darkness of the page will help making them appear less abrasive.
